Hi Ritwick, 
 
No, so far we are only considering the counts. We are essentially calculating the ratio between the intronic and exonic counts for each gene in each individual, and then checking whether the ratio of this ratio between conditions is different from 1. As such, any consistent (even gene-specific) biases should already cancel out in this division. There might be a problem if the biases are not consistent among samples, but we would have to see how to actually correct for that without overfitting or introducing new artifacts. In any case it is probably useful to look at these aspects in an exploratory fashion, to see whether they seem to have anything to do with the observed patterns. I will also say that Salmon incorporates several bias terms in their estimation of transcript abundances, so it could also be interesting to see whether the abundances thus obtained would be different from the current set of counts. 
 
Charlotte

In both cases we use size factors taken from exonic counts and follow your overall strategy.
